Which Productions Write Film History? Measuring Artistic Influence Based on Network Centrality

Wednesdays@NICO Seminar | 12:00-1:00 PM, January 21, 2015 | Chambers Hall, Lower Level

Agnes Horvat, Postdoctoral Fellow, Northwestern Institute on Complex Systems (NICO), Northwestern University

Abstract

Currently lacking in film and performing art is the systematic quantitative measurement of the concept of artistic influence and expression. In this study, I propose and validate a new measure of artwork-to-artwork expression. Based on an original data set of the network of artistic influence drawn from over 40,000 international feature films dating from the birth of cinema, and garnered from crowdsourced platforms, I develop a measure of cinematic influences that determines the most inspiring creations in world cinema. Further, I validate the measure by comparing the identified influential films to various existing lists of 'greatest' films, such as personal favorite lists and lists deduced from expert polls.

Bio

Ágnes Horvát is a postdoctoral researcher at the Northwestern Institute on Complex Systems. She earned her PhD in Interdisciplinary Physics from Heidelberg University, Germany in 2013, a BSc in Physics and Computer Science (Babes-Bolyai University, Romania), and a BA in Photography, Film, and Media (Sapientia Hungarian University, Romania). Her research focuses on developing theory and methods at the intersection of physics, computer science and social phenomena. Her current work spans the areas of collective intelligence, crowdfunding, and the creative industries. Besides her research, she has a passion for blending science and the arts.
